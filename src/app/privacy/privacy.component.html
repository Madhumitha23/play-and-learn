COMPLIANCE WITH CHILDREN’S ONLINE PRIVACY PROTECTION ACT (“COPPA”)


GoodChildrenGames complies with the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (“COPPA”), a U.S. law designed to protect the online privacy of children. Whenever we refer to “children” in this Privacy Policy, we mean children under the age of 13, unless otherwise indicated. We take precautions designed to protect the online privacy of children, and we encourage parents to take an active role in protecting their children’s privacy online by knowing and understanding information that is collected.


GoodChildrenGames does not knowingly collect personal information as defined by COPPA from children. If we learn or have reason to suspect that we have collected personal information from a child in violation of COPPA, we will promptly delete it.


If you have any questions or comments about how we treat children’s information or our compliance with COPPA, please contact us as set out in the CONTACT INFORMATION section below. If you would like to learn more about COPPA, you can refer to the Children’s Privacy section of the Federal Trade Commission’s website here:

WHAT INFORMATION DO WE COLLECT?


1. We collect information from you if you “Contact” us to ask us a question or send us a comment. We may reply to you to answer your question or respond to your comment on a one-time basis. Any contact made by a child will be deleted once we respond to your question or comment, and we will not re-use it to re-contact the child.


2. We use a third-party advertising partner, Playwire Media, to serve ads when you visit our website. Playwire offers a kidSAFE+ certified COPPA compliant advertising platform for publishers of child-directed websites. Through Playwire, only contextually-based ads are served to our visitors. These ads are NOT shown based on a user’s browsing history or by behavioral targeting. While these ads don’t use cookies for ad personalization, they may use cookies to allow for frequency capping, aggregated ad reporting, and to combat fraud and abuse.


3. We use Google Analytics as a web analysis tool that helps us understand how visitors engage with our website. Google Analytics uses first-party cookies to track visitor interactions. The Google Analytics privacy policy can be found here.
